## Key Legal Points from California&#39;s Bureau of Gambling Control

- California is one of the strictest states for gambling&#x2F;slot machines
- all controlled games&#x2F;activities \(jackpots&#x2F;bonuses&#x2F;drawings\) must be approved in writing by the Division of Gambling Control before users can play at a licensed card room
- Slot machines are **only legal at tribal casinos**
  - tribal casinos = owned &amp; operated by Native American Tribes \(ex: Pechanga Casino\)
  - tribal casinos exempted b&#x2F;c recognized as own **soverign nations**
- if the app involves **no real money**, it is classified as social&#x2F;entertainment gaming
- both **state AND local ordinances** must be complied with
- owning a physical slot machine is **ILLEGAL in CA** unless its 25\+yrs old and not used for gambling
- **UIGEA** \(Unlawful Internet Gambling Enforcement Act\) governs online real\-money gambling federally \(if our app considers real money features\)
- sweepstakes casino loophole banned Jan 2026
  - users played for free but could redeem virtual coins for real cash prizes\. since coins could be claimed for free, it was legally classified as a **sweepstakes**\. in reality, users were **buying coins**, so it was functioning as a real casino
  - Gov Newsom banned this so free\-to\-play apps could exit but **cannot** offer **real cash prizes** or redemptions

## What makes a slot machine legally a &quot;slot machine&quot;

- any device that determines win&#x2F;loss after a bet electronically counts
- CA Penal Code includes software&#x2F;app&#x2F;electronic circuit that contains code with no other use than a slot machine qualifies

## State by State Context

- Nevada is one of the few states where all slot machines are fully legal
  - but free\-to\-play apps are banend
- regulation varies depending on state
- user in Nevada vs CA has different relationship with slot machines
- if we want to add real\-money features, need to enforce restrictions by state
- free\-to\-play is the least restricted model without heavy legal implications
- social casino apps are **prohibited** in **Washington, Idaho, and Nevada**

## Age Restrictions

- social&#x2F;free to play casino apps enforce 18\+&#x2F;21\+ age restriction as best practice
- app store &amp; google play require appropriate age ratings for gambling\-themed apps
- App Store requires 17\+ rating for all social casino apps; must be labeled 21\+ and for amusement purposes only

## Gambling Disclosures

- online gambling apps **must clearly state** that the app **does not** offer **real\-money prizes** or the opportunity to win real money
  - must be stated in app itself and in any descriptions
- 35&#x2F;38 US gambling jurisdictions require problem gambling hotline numbers to be displayed
  - CA&#39;s mandated resource is `problemgambling\.ca\.gov`

## FTC Dark Patterns

- FTC = US gov agency responsible for consumer protection &amp; prevents deceptive business practices
- dark patterns = UI&#x2F;UX design choices built to manipulate users into doing something they don&#39;t intend to do
- manipulative uI design is legally actionable under federal consumer protection law
  - ex: near\-miss effects, autoplay, urgency timers should be **avoided** for a free\-to\-play app
