# Slot Machine Jargon&#x2F;Terminology

Common slot machine terms and phrases that may appear in our research, design discussions, user stories, and AI prompts\.

## Core terms

### Slot machine

A game where the player spins reels and wins when certain symbol combinations appear according to the game’s rules\.

### Reel

A vertical column of symbols that spins during gameplay\. Traditional machines often have 3 reels, while modern digital slots commonly use 5 or more\.

### Row

A horizontal position across the reels\. For example, a 5x3 slot has 5 reels and 3 visible rows\.

### Grid

The full visible layout of symbols on the screen, usually described as reels x rows \(such as 5x3\)\.

### Symbol

An image or icon shown on a reel\. Symbols form winning or losing combinations\.

### Spin

One play of the game\. The player presses a button, the reels move, and the result is determined\.

### Bet

The amount of money or credits the player wagers on one spin\.

### Credit

A unit used to represent the player’s balance in the game\. In real\-money gambling slots, credits map to money\. In entertainment&#x2F;mobile slots, they are often fake currency\.

### Coin Value &#x2F; Denomination

The value of each credit\. Some games let the player change this to affect wager size\.

## Winning Mechanics

### Payline

A specific pattern across the reels that can produce a win if the right symbols appear in the required order\. Older slots often had a fixed number of paylines; modern ones may have many\.

### Active Payline

A payline currently enabled for the spin\. In some games, players choose how many paylines to activate\.

### Winning Combination

A set of symbols that matches a payout rule in the game\.

### Paytable

A chart or menu showing symbol values, winning combinations, bonus symbols, and special rules\.

### Left\-to\-Right Win

A system where matching symbols must begin from the leftmost reel and continue rightward\.

### Right\-to\-Left Win

A less common system where wins can also start from the rightmost reel\.

### Ways to Win

Instead of fixed paylines, some slots award wins for matching symbols on adjacent reels regardless of exact row position\. Example: “243 ways” or “1024 ways\.”

### Cluster Pays

A win system where players win by landing groups of matching symbols that touch each other, instead of matching symbols along paylines\.

### Scatter Pay

A win system where certain symbols pay anywhere on the screen, without needing to land on a payline\.

## Special Symbols

### Wild

A symbol that can substitute for other symbols to help complete winning combinations\.

### Expanding Wild

A wild that grows to cover extra positions, often an entire reel\.

### Stacked Symbol

A symbol that appears across multiple positions on the same reel\.

### Scatter

A symbol that usually triggers bonuses or free spins and often does not need to appear on a payline\.

### Bonus Symbol

A symbol that activates a bonus feature, minigame, or special round\.

### Multiplier

A feature that increases winnings by a set amount, such as 2x, 3x, or 10x\.

## Bonus Features

### Free Spins

A bonus mode where the player gets a set number of spins without paying the normal bet cost\.

### Re\-Spin

A feature that lets some or all reels spin again after the original result\.

### Cascading Reels &#x2F; Avalanche

When winning symbols disappear and new symbols fall into place, possibly creating additional wins in the same spin\.

### Bonus Round

A separate gameplay section triggered by certain symbols or conditions\. It may include picking items, wheel spins, or other extra mechanics\.

### Progressive Jackpot

A jackpot that increases over time as more players play, until someone wins it\.

### Fixed Jackpot

A jackpot with a set value that does not grow dynamically\.

### Mini &#x2F; Minor &#x2F; Major &#x2F; Grand

Common jackpot tiers used to show different prize levels\.

## Probability and Fairness Terms

### RNG \(Random Number Generator\)

The algorithm that determines game outcomes randomly\. In regulated gambling slots, the RNG is essential for fairness\.

### Random Outcome

Each spin result is determined independently and is not supposed to be affected by previous spins\.

### House Edge

The mathematical advantage the game operator has over the player in the long run\.

### RTP \(Return to Player\)

The theoretical percentage of wagered money a slot returns to players over a very large number of spins\. For example, 96% RTP means the game is designed to return about $96 for every $100 wagered over time, on average\.

### Volatility &#x2F; Variance

Describes how a slot tends to pay out:

- **Low volatility:** smaller wins more often
- **High volatility:** bigger wins less often

### Hit Frequency

How often the game gives any win at all, even a small one\.

## Common UI &#x2F; Product Terms

### Spin Button

The main control used to start a spin\.

### Auto\-Spin

A feature that automatically performs multiple spins in a row\. This may be restricted or regulated in some places\.

### Balance

The player’s remaining credits or money\.

### Win Display

The section of the UI showing how much was won on the current spin\.

### Max Bet

A control that sets the wager to the highest allowed amount\.

### Turbo Spin &#x2F; Quick Spin

A faster animation mode that shortens spin time\.

### Session

One continuous period of play by a user\.

### Daily Bonus

A reward given for returning to the game each day, common in entertainment&#x2F;mobile slot apps\.

### Currency Pack &#x2F; Coin Pack

An in\-app purchase that gives the player more fake currency or credits\.

## Mobile &#x2F; Entertainment Slot Terms

### Social Casino

A slot or casino\-style app that uses fake currency instead of real\-money gambling, often focused on entertainment and retention features\.

### Fake Currency &#x2F; Virtual Currency

Coins, gems, or chips that do not directly cash out into real money\.

### Reward Loop

A design pattern where rewards, sounds, animations, and progression systems encourage continued play\.

### Retention Mechanic

A feature meant to bring users back, such as daily rewards, missions, streaks, or unlockable content\.

### Cosmetic Theme

The visual style of the game, such as Vegas, fruit machine, fantasy, neon, pirate, or AI\-themed\.

## User Experience and Design Terms

### Feedback

The visual, audio, or motion response the system gives after a player action\.

### Juice

Extra polish such as sound effects, particles, bounce animations, and satisfying transitions that make interactions feel better\.

### Onboarding

The first\-time user experience that teaches players how to use the game\.

### Accessibility

Design choices that make the game usable for more people, such as readable text, color contrast, reduced motion, and clear button labels\.

### Responsive Layout

A UI that adapts well to different screen sizes and device types\.
