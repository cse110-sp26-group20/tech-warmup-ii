# Types of users\(spectrum of experience\)

## Perceived Control

**Novice Players:**

- New players often approach slot machines with an implicit belief that outcomes are partially controllable\. This manifests in ideas like “timing the spin” or choosing machines that feel “ready to pay\.” These beliefs are not grounded in how RNG systems work but significantly shape engagement and emotional investment\.

**Experienced Players:**

- More experienced users typically understand that outcomes are governed by Random Number Generators and are statistically independent\. However, many still retain _soft heuristics_ \(e\.g\., preferring certain machines or denominations\), reflecting a partial—not complete—transition to probabilistic thinking\.

## Emotional Response

**Novice Players:**<br> Highly reactive to:

- Near\-miss outcomes
- Small wins \(even net losses\)
- Sensory feedback \(lights, sounds, animations\)

**Experienced Players:**

- Reduced sensitivity to near\-misses
- More stable emotional responses
- Engagement shifts from excitement → rhythm and habit

## Behavioral Patterns &amp; Session Dynamics

**Novice Players:**

- Lower initial stakes but less structured play
- Higher likelihood of “chasing losses”
- Sessions driven by emotion rather than planning

**Experienced Players:**

- More consistent bet sizing
- Defined session limits \(time or money\)
- Greater emphasis on pacing and longevity
